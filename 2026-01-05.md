### Song: Reggae Bring Back Love

### Artist: Morgan Heritage

### Year oF production: 1999

### Genre: Reggae Roots

Reggae bring back love is a Song by a popular Grammy winning Jamaican-American roots
reggae band, often dubbed the “Royal Family of Reggae,”(due to their dynastic legacy of reggae artistry passed from father to children).

They have been relevant for the longest time due to their ability for their music to blend roots reggae traditions with modern influences
helping keep reggae relevant across generations.

In their songs, they emphasize family unity, spiritual messages, and cultural pride, which resonate deeply with reggae audiences worldwide. Formed in 1994 in Brooklyn,
New York, by five siblings of renowned reggae artist Denroy Morgan known for the 1981 hit “I’ll Do Anything for You”.
The song is part of the album Don't Haffi Dread.

The siblings behind the melodious voices of Morgan heritage are;

    •	Peter “Peetah” Morgan who has been the lead in vocals
    •	Roy “Gramps” Morgan known for vocals and keyboard.
    •	Una Morgan also for vocals and keyboard
    •	Nakhamyah “Lukes” Morgan for vocals
    •	Memmalatel “Mr. Mojo” Morgan for vocals

While there are no hidden meanings behind the lyrics, it is important to note that the band basically focused on blending music, culture and traditions/modernity.
