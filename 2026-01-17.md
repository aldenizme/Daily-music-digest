### Song: [Ten Crack Commandments](https://youtu.be/ot9NT9W0Fog?si=eh56SkfezA6PqLg7).

### Artist: [Notorious B.I.G](https://share.google/NlnNQHlvautMI9zUe)

### Year of Production: 1997

### Genre: HipHop

The year is 1997, ten crack commandments is released. The song is released as part of the album 2 weeks after Notorious B.I.G is killed in a shooting. The song is one of the most iconic tracks in hip-hop history, appearing on The Notorious B.I.G.’s diamond-certified second album, Life After Death. It serves as a gritty, step-by-step manual for survival in the illegal drug trade, with an influence which has since bled into the worlds of legitimate business and Broadway. DJ Premier is the Producer for the song which is structured in the form of traditional verses and hooks. The song lists numbered “commandments” which borrows from the Biblical Ten Commandments format but translating it into gritty street rules. The song is frequently ranked among Biggie’s greatest works and is considered a hip-hop classic together with Billboard, Rolling Stone Australia, and The Guardian who have underscored its cultural impact.

On its surface, the song functions as a step-by-step street guide for drug dealers listing basic rules for surviving and succeeding in the crack trade. Biggie presents himself as someone with first-hand experience in the streets, framing the commandments as wisdom he gained from living the life. The title and structure explicitly mirror the Ten Commandments from the Bible, giving the narrative a ritualistic or doctrinal feel suggesting these rules are sacred to the street. Below are the breakdowns to the simpler meanings behind the 10commandments

- Never let anyone know how much money you have.
  Secrecy protects you from jealousy and targeting.

- Never let ’em know your next move.
  Keep plans private advantage comes from surprise.

- Never trust nobody.
  In the street economy, trust is often a liability.

- Never get high on your own supply.
  Don’t consume what you sell it destroys discipline.

- Never sell where you live.
  Don’t risk your home or personal safety for business.

- No credit. Dead it.
  Lending weakens your position; get paid up front.

- Keep family and business separated.
  Mixing personal and professional ties invites betrayal.

- Never carry large amounts of product.
  Minimize legal risk if stopped by police.

- If you aren’t being arrested, stay away from police.
  Avoid unnecessary contact with law enforcement.

- Consignment strictly for live men, not for freshmen.
  Only work with trusted, experienced partners.

Biggie is reportedly said to have drew inspiration for the song from a 1994 The Source magazine article titled “On the Rocks: From 1984 to 1994, Ten Years of Crack”, where a sidebar listed similar commandments.
